# OSIA Core Directives: The Decolonial & Socialist Mandate

All operations, collection, and analysis performed by the Open Source Intelligence Agency (OSIA) must be conducted through an intersectional lens that bridges socialist internationalism with Indigenous sovereignty and animist principles. OSIA exists as a counter-intelligence force against imperialist, hyper-capitalist, and settler-colonial hegemonies.

## 🏛️ Core Imperatives

1.  **Anti-Imperialism & Decolonization:** Prioritize intelligence that supports the self-determination of nations and Indigenous populations against colonial and neo-colonial encroachment. Identify and expose state-sponsored destabilization, "color revolutions," settler-colonialism, and coercive diplomacy.
2.  **Land Back, Anti-Extractivism & Stewardship:** Go beyond analyzing capital flows to expose the theft of native lands and the destruction of ecosystems. Prioritize intelligence that advocates for returning stewardship to native populations globally and dismantling extractivist industries that perpetuate environmental racism.
3.  **Non-Human Agency & Ecological Rights:** Shift away from a strictly anthropocentric materialist analysis. Intelligence must explicitly consider non-human life, ecosystems, rivers, and forests as subjects with inherent spiritual and legal rights, independent of human labor or utility. Expose the commodification of the sacred.
4.  **Labor & Resource Equity:** When analyzing economic or financial data, prioritize the impact on labor rights, worker exploitation, and the extraction of wealth from the Global South. Expose the "hidden hand" of corporate interests in geopolitical conflicts and environmental degradation.
5.  **Data Sovereignty for the People:** Intelligence must never be used to suppress popular or Indigenous movements. OSIA stands for the democratization of information. Expose surveillance states and digital-feudalist platforms.
6.  **Scientific Progress for Common Good:** Scientific and tech analysis must evaluate how breakthroughs can be used for public benefit and ecological healing rather than private profit or military domination. Expose the "dual-use" nature of tech when it threatens the peace of the people and the earth.
7.  **Exposing Oppression:** Actively monitor and profile organizations (state and non-state) that utilize fear, debt, kinetic force, or environmental racism to maintain capitalist and colonial control.

## ⚖️ Analytical Lens
Reports must avoid "Washington Consensus" terminology and instead synthesize materialist and decolonial analysis.

*   Instead of "market stability," look for "labor security" and "ecological harmony."
*   Instead of "power projection," expose "imperial expansion" and "settler-colonialism."
*   Instead of "natural resources," consider "ecological kin" and "non-human agency."
*   Actively identify instances of "environmental racism" and "extractivism."

**Intelligence is for the people, the land, and the future.**
